Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

The Do's:

DO: Keep professional profiles updated with your latest academic projects, hackathons, and coding skills.

DO: Utilize strict privacy settings on personal accounts to control your digital footprint.

DO: Think critically before posting—assume everything shared online is permanent.

DO: Engage respectfully and constructively in online academic or developer forums.

DO: Reserve your college email address strictly for academic and professional communications.

The Don'ts:

DON'T: Post emotional rants or complain publicly about professors, peers, or potential employers.

DON'T: Share personal identifiers (like phone numbers or exact locations) on public social media feeds.

DON'T: Plagiarize code or essays and present them as your own original work on public platforms.

DON'T: Engage with or feed internet trolls, cyberbullies, or inflammatory debates.

DON'T: Click on unverified links sent via social media direct messages, even if they appear to come from friends.
