Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist for Students

Financial/UPI Safety: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money; PINs are exclusively required for sending money or checking your balance.

Financial/UPI Safety: Always double-check the recipient's VPA (Virtual Payment Address) and the exact amount on the screen before authorizing any payment.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important academic, personal, and social media accounts.

Do not connect to public, unsecured Wi-Fi networks at cafes or airports when logging into bank accounts or making transactions.

Keep your laptop and mobile operating systems, as well as antivirus software, updated to the latest versions to patch security vulnerabilities.

Be highly skeptical of urgent emails, messages, or phone calls demanding immediate action, personal information, or passwords.

Use a reliable password manager to generate and store complex, unique passwords for every different website or service.
8. Reporting Channel: If you encounter suspicious online activity or fall victim to financial fraud, report it immediately to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.
