Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Request Fraud Targeting College Students

What the crime is: Unified Payments Interface (UPI) fraud involves malicious actors deceiving individuals into authorizing unauthorized money transfers from their bank accounts using deceptive payment requests on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.

How it typically happens: In a common realistic scenario, a college student lists a used textbook or bicycle for sale on an online marketplace. The scammer contacts the student, posing as an eager buyer who agrees to the asking price immediately. The scammer claims they cannot pay in cash and will send the money via a UPI app. However, instead of sending the funds, the scammer sends a "payment request" (the collect feature) or a malicious QR code. Using high-pressure tactics or claiming there is a network issue, they instruct the student to "enter your UPI PIN to receive the funds."

Who is usually targeted: College students in India are frequent targets because they regularly use digital payment apps for small, rapid transactions like splitting bills, and they often buy or sell used academic goods online.

The consequences: If the student enters their UPI PIN, the specified amount is instantly deducted from their own bank account and transferred to the scammer. Because UPI transactions are immediate and bank-to-bank, reversing the transaction is highly difficult. This results in an unrecoverable direct financial loss for the student, which can be devastating for someone on a strict college budget.
